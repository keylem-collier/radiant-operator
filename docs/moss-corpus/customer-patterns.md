# Customer Patterns

## The intake bottleneck pattern (very common)

Service business owners say they need more leads. Underneath, they fear more leads will break their front desk, intake team, or follow-up process.

What they actually want: controlled growth — enough demand they can handle without chaos.

Demo line: "We cannot have the ads team pushing aggressive lead volume if our ops team is still fixing intake."

## Dental client specifics

- Front desk overwhelm is the emotional fear, not price.
- They have been burned by agencies promising volume.
- Prior promise example: do not scale spend until intake bottleneck is fixed.

## Objection: "Will this replace our team?"

Answer frame: this gives their team memory and leverage, not replacement. AI handles the messy context; humans keep the relationship.

## Objection: "How fast will we see leads?"

Redirect to readiness: if intake is broken, leads fast is a liability. Phase one is fixing the path from click to booked appointment.

## Northstar Dental (fictionalized demo client)

- Ops lead was out last week — CRM changes paused until Friday.
- Intake team overwhelmed — do not promise volume spike.
- Positioning: phased growth, intake first, then scale Meta spend.

## Creative guardrails

- Do not sell raw lead volume when ops is the constraint.
- Do not use fear-mongering creative — use empathy + control.
- TikTok hooks should feel native, not like a corporate explainer.
