# Meta Ads Strategy Notes

## Creative that converts for service businesses

Lead with the pain, not the platform. Meta users scroll fast — your hook has three seconds to stop the thumb.

For dental, home services, and local operators, the winning angle is rarely "more leads." It is "fix the thing breaking after the lead arrives."

## Common mistakes

- Selling volume when the client's front desk is drowning.
- Using stock photos of smiling dentists instead of showing the real bottleneck.
- Running broad targeting before the offer and landing page are tight.
- Ignoring frequency — if the same person sees your ad 8 times with a weak hook, you are burning trust.

## Account structure (keep it simple for demos)

- One campaign per offer angle, not per platform gimmick.
- Creative testing beats audience hacking at early spend levels.
- Retargeting only works if the first-touch message was clear.

## Hooks that work for intake-bottleneck clients

- "More leads won't help if your front desk is drowning."
- "Your ads aren't broken. Your follow-up is."
- "Stop buying traffic you can't absorb."

## What to say in sales calls

Position phased growth: fix intake, then scale spend. Never promise raw lead volume when ops is the constraint.
